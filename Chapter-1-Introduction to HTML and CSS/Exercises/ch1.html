Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. It's simple, yet rich and delicious!

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt
  • 2 cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Method

  1. Put a large saucepan of water on to boil.
  2. Finely chop the pancetta. Beat the eggs in a medium bowl, season with a little freshly grated black pepper and set everything aside.
  3. Add 1 tsp salt to the boiling water, add the spaghetti and cook according to the packet instructions.
  4. Squash the garlic with the blade of a knife, just to bruise it.
  5. While the spaghetti is cooking, fry the pancetta with the garlic. Drop the garlic into the pan, along with the pancetta. Leave to cook on a medium heat for about 5 minutes, stirring often, until the pancetta is golden and crisp.
  6. Keep the heat under the pancetta on low. When the pasta is ready, lift it from the water with a pasta fork or tongs and put it in the frying pan with the pancetta. Don't worry if a little water drops in the pan.
  7. Mix most of the cheese in with the eggs, keeping a small handful back for sprinkling over later.
  8. Take the pan of spaghetti and pancetta off the heat. Quickly pour in the eggs and cheese. Using the tongs or a long fork, lift up the spaghetti so it mixes easily with the egg mixture, which thickens but doesn't scramble, and everything is coated.
  9. Serve immediately with a sprinkling of the remaining cheese and a grating of black pepper. If the dish gets too dry, add a little pasta cooking water.
